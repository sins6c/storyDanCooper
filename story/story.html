The Story of Dan Cooper

ALERT

The "Dan Cooper" plane hijacking, more commonly known as the "D.B. Cooper" case, is one of the most intriguing unsolved mysteries in American history

On November 24, 1971, a man who identified himself as Dan Cooper (later mistakenly referred to as D.B. Cooper) boarded a Northwest Orient Airlines flight from Portland, Oregon to Seattle, Washington. Cooper was described as being in his mid-40s, wearing a business suit with a black tie and white shirt. He sat in the rear of the plane and ordered a drink.

During the flight, Cooper handed a note to one of the flight attendants, Florence Schaffner, which stated that he had a bomb and demanded $200,000 in cash, four parachutes, and a fuel truck standing by in Seattle to refuel the plane upon arrival. Cooper showed Schaffner a briefcase that he claimed contained the bomb.

Once the plane landed in Seattle, Cooper released the passengers in exchange for the ransom and the parachutes. He kept several crew members on board and instructed the pilots to fly to Mexico City at a low altitude. Somewhere over southwestern Washington state, Cooper opened the rear stairs of the plane and parachuted out into the night, disappearing without a trace.

Despite extensive searches by law enforcement and the FBI, no sign of Cooper or the majority of the ransom money was ever found. Over the years, there have been numerous theories and suspects, but the case remains unsolved. The FBI officially closed its investigation in 2016, stating that they had exhausted all leads.

However, many believe that Dan Cooper may still be out there somewhere. His family and friends continue to search for him, hoping against hope that he is alive and will one day come home

The hijacking of Northwest Orient Airlines Flight 305 by a man who came to be known as D.B. Cooper is a captivating tale that has intrigued investigators, armchair detectives, and the public alike for over five decades. On that fateful Thanksgiving Eve in 1971, Flight 305 took off from Portland International Airport bound for Seattle-Tacoma International Airport. Little did anyone on board know that they were about to become part of one of the most baffling mysteries in aviation history.

The D.B. Cooper case has captivated the public imagination for decades, inspiring books, movies, and endless speculation about the fate of the mysterious hijacker.
